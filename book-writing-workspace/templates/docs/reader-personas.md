# Reader Personas

Define the intended readers before expanding the manuscript. Replace every placeholder.

## Primary Persona

- **Role / background**: (Who is the main reader?)
- **Prior knowledge**: (What can this reader already explain or do?)
- **Use context**: (Why and where will this reader use the book?)
- **Pain points**: (What currently blocks or confuses this reader?)
- **Completion outcome**: (What should this reader be able to decide or do after reading?)
- **Explanation depth**: (What belongs in the main flow, and what should be deferred?)

## Secondary Personas

### Secondary Persona 1

- **Role / background**: (Who else should benefit?)
- **Prior knowledge**: (What can this reader already explain or do?)
- **Completion outcome**: (What should this reader gain without displacing the primary persona?)

## Non-Goals

- (Readers or advanced use cases this book does not optimize for)
- (Details that belong in another book, appendix, or reference)

## Review Lens

- Can the primary persona follow the main argument without unstated prerequisites?
- Does each chapter stop at the depth this persona needs at that point?
- Are advanced settings, exceptions, and alternate procedures deferred to the chapter where they are used?
- Do secondary-persona examples support rather than interrupt the main flow?
- Does the final manuscript deliver the stated completion outcome?
